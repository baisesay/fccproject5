Bai Bureh

The man who fought against the British for his country Sierra Leone

Bai Bureh seen here in 1898 in Kasseh, sitting relaxed in his traditional dress with a handkerchief in his hands, while a guard stands next to him

Here is a time line of Bai Bureh

  • 1840 - Born in Kasseh, a village near PortLoko in Northern Sierra Leone.
  • 1860s & 1870s - He become the top warrior of PortLoko and the entire Northern Sierra Leone.
  • 1882 - Bai Bureh fought against the Susu People from French Guinea now called Guinea who invaded Kambia, a town in Northern Sierra Leone.
  • 1893 - Bai Bureh refused to recognise the hut tax that the British had imposed. He did not believe the Sierra Leonean people had a duty to pay taxes to foreigners abd he wanted all British to return to Britain and let Sierra Leonean slove their own problems
  • 1898 - He declared war on the British in Sierra Leone, the war later became know as the Hut Tax War. but he later surrenderd on November 11, when he was tracked down in a swampy, thickly vegetated countryside by a small patrolling party of the newly organised West Africa Regiment in Port Loko. The British sent him in exile to the Gold Coast (now Ghana).
  • 1905 - Bai Bureh was brought back to Sierra Leone and reinstated as the Chief of Kasseh.
  • 1908 - Died at the age of 68
